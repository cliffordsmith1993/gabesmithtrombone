Bio

Gabe Smith

Gabriel Smith is a trombonist and private teacher currently residing in north central Oklahoma. Gabe recently completed his graduate studies at Oklahoma State University (MM '17) under the tutelage of Paul Compton at Oklahoma State University. Before moving to Oklahoma to pursue his master's degree, Gabe studied with Timothy Riordan at VanderCook College of Music in Chicago (BM '15).

While at Oklahoma State, Gabe performed with various ensembles including the symphony orchestra (principal trombone), symphonic band (principal trombone), jazz ensemble (lead trombone), trombone octet, trombone quartet, and jazzbones. Gabe also conducted the trombone choir and taught private lessons to undergraduate students, a privilege available through a graduate assistantship.

While in Chicago, Gabe studied music education coursework at VanderCook and performed with the VanderCook wind ensemble, orchestra, and jazz band, as well as the Chicago Classical Symphony Orchestra. Gabe also had the privilege of performing as a featured soloist with the VanderCook trombone choir. Gabe also performed with the VanderCook Symphonic Band at the Midwest clinic from 2011-2014.

Gabe's freelance experience includes playing in sacred settings, brass quintets, and pit orchestras for musicals such as the Music Man, Damn Yankees!, Crazy for You, and Company. While playing with the Classical Symphony Orchestra, Gabe performed major works and symphonies by composers such as Beethoven, Mahler, Tchaikovsky, Nielsen, Debussy, and Mussorgsky.
