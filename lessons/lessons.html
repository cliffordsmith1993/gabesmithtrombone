One-on-One Learning

Students who are studying individually are able to take 30 minute or 60 minute lessons. During this time we will address fundamental trombone technicians including articulation, breathing, and embouchure. We will also study etudes and solo repertoire which will be contoured to each student's needs and goals. Examples of goals a student may have that I can assist them in achieving could be making regional or all-state band, performing a piece at an end of the year recital, developing good practice techniques, and ensemble playing. Parents, directors, and students alike, please feel free to contact.me with any questions you may have or for a phone or email consultation.

Ensemble Coaching

It is a belief of mine and every successful teacher I've known that playing chamber music is one of the best things musicians can be involved in. By bringing individuals with different abilities together to practice and perform music, students will learn how to work with one another, how to bring their individual best to reach a collective goal, and the joy that comes with group music making.

I offer quartet and sectional coaching. Quartets can be composed of students from the same or different schools. Sectional coaching time and location will be determined by the director if desired. Parents, students, and directors, please reach out if you or a trombonist you know may be interested in participating in a quartet. Sectional coaching is available for high and low brass.

Why should I Invest in Lessons?

Music provides a creative outlet and craft for students to pursue. Whether or not my students ultimately end up making a career out of music or play as a hobby I attempt to build a healthy and transferable growth mindset when I am teaching. I believe hard work trumps natural talent every time. The discipline required to study a musical instrument is great but not out of reach for anyone truly willing to put in the time. By studying an instrument and constantly putting work in towards a consistent goal students are also learning the discipline to pursue other goals they may have.

When one invests in something they expect a return. Being a musician, I am biased to believe the skill and experience is reason enough, students who intend to go to college will find a wider range of scholarship options open to them as a trained musician. Many Texas residents have attained an out-of-state tuition waiver by participating in marching band while attending Oklahoma State University (a waiver of $7,000, more than half of the out of state tuition of $11,000 for the 2016-17 academic year).
